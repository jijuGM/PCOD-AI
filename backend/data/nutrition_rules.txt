Eating protein before carbohydrates can reduce blood sugar spikes and may reduce cravings for sweets.

High-protein snacks such as Greek yogurt, nuts, eggs, or cottage cheese can help stabilize hunger levels.

Foods with a low glycemic index are recommended for individuals with PCOD because they cause slower increases in blood sugar levels.

Whole grains such as oats, quinoa, and brown rice are better carbohydrate choices compared to refined grains.

Refined sugars and processed foods can cause rapid blood sugar spikes followed by crashes, which may trigger cravings.

Fiber-rich foods such as vegetables, legumes, and whole grains help improve insulin sensitivity.

Healthy fats such as avocado, olive oil, nuts, and seeds can improve satiety and reduce overeating.

Eating balanced meals every 3–4 hours can help prevent blood sugar drops and reduce intense cravings.

Dark chocolate with high cocoa content (70% or more) can be used as a controlled indulgence when craving sweets.

Fruit paired with protein (for example apple with peanut butter) can provide a balanced snack.

Hydration should be maintained throughout the day because mild dehydration can trigger hunger signals.

Portion control is important when consuming high-calorie foods, even when they are healthy.

Instead of eliminating all indulgent foods, planned small indulgence portions may reduce binge eating.

Protein-rich breakfasts can reduce cravings later in the day.

Eating slowly and mindfully can improve satiety signals and prevent overeating.

Avoid long periods of fasting unless medically supervised, because it can increase hunger hormones.

Evening snacks should ideally include protein and fiber to prevent late-night cravings.

Regular meal timing helps regulate metabolism and stabilize hunger patterns.

A balanced diet including vegetables, lean protein, whole grains, and healthy fats is recommended for managing PCOD symptoms.
